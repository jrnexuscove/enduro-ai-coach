# Ground Truth: Colin Hill

## 1. Rider objective & context
The rider is attempting a hill climb on a dry, rocky woodland hill. Third-person filming.

## 2. Rider
Remains seated the entire clip. Throttle is on throughout. After the first corner, he starts paddling with his feet as the hill steepens and momentum fades. Weight stays on the seat — never transitions to standing.

## 3. Terrain & environment
Dry, rocky woodland hill. Visible loose rocks, sandy/dusty/soily material, tree roots. Not a straight line — the path winds through trees. Includes a berm at the beginning.

## 4. Events
- Rider comes into a berm at speed
- Takes a right-hand turn
- Begins a left-hand turn going uphill — this is where trouble starts
- Still seated, back wheel spinning heavily
- Hill gets steeper with rocks in the way
- Starts paddling with feet
- Loses momentum progressively
- Bails off the bike

## 5. Outcome
Bail — rider loses momentum on the climb and comes off the bike. Not a crash, a controlled-ish bail from loss of momentum.

## 6. Unclear or not visible
There are some points where the rider passes behind two trees and visibility is briefly lost. Camera then zooms in and rider is clearly visible again.
